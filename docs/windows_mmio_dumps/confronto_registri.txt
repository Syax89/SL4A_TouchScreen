Key observation: CTRL0 differs ONLY in the byte at bits 8-15 (Windows=0x0E, Linux=0xA9).
All other CTRL0 bytes match exactly during touch. This byte is the most likely
starting point for understanding why the write works on Windows and not on Linux.

Methodological note: the touch1/2/3 dumps are identical to each other (except for a counter
in STATUS offset 0x4E), so they probably capture the "settled" post-transfer state rather than
the exact transient during the write. Capturing the real transient would require a hardware
breakpoint in the kernel debugger (requires a second machine) or a much higher-frequency dump.
